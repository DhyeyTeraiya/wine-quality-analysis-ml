Alcohol is King (23.1% importance)

Wines scoring 7–8 have avg alcohol 11.5% vs 9.8% for low-quality wines. The ML model ranks alcohol as the #1 predictor of wine quality.

⚠️

Volatile Acidity is the Enemy

High volatile acidity creates a vinegar-like taste. Quality 3–4 wines average VA of 0.73 g/L vs 0.41 g/L for quality 7–8 wines.

🎯

Class Imbalance Challenge

Only 1.1% (18 wines) score 8+, while 43% score either 5 or 6. SMOTE oversampling was used to handle imbalanced training data.

💡

Optimal Wine Formula

Best wines share: Alcohol > 11%, Sulphates 0.7–1.2, Volatile Acidity < 0.4, Citric Acid > 0.3. These are the 4 levers for quality wine production.
